About Brain Strokes

A brain stroke, also known as a cerebrovascular accident (CVA), is a medical condition that occurs when the blood supply to a part of the brain is interrupted or reduced. This can lead to damage to brain cells and potential disability or death.

Brain strokes can have various causes, including high blood pressure, heart diseases, and lifestyle factors. Prevention strategies involve maintaining a healthy lifestyle, managing risk factors, and seeking medical advice.

If you have concerns about brain strokes or your risk factors, consult with a healthcare professional for personalized guidance and advice.
